1 - Welcome aboard!

10/11/2024

Music composition might sound like something completely unreachable to the average person. You might think that you need expensive software and hardware, and years of knowledge in music theory, sound design, mixing and mastering. But the truth is: it is no harder to pick than learning to draw. Drawing can get extremely complex with advanced theory requiring many tools. Still, any child can start drawing given a paper and a pencil. And, like drawing, it can be an enjoyable activity no matter how experienced you are. After a bit of basic knowledge in place, you can then, in the same way, explore many styles, which in music we call genres. And that's what make it so interesting. There is always room for exploration, for learning new things, for trying new things.
That's why I picked this subject: to make it more accessible, and because you can easily adjust it to your taste.

This is the challenge that I want you to try. Even if you never did music, or have no knowledge about music theory. Because while everyone knows what music is, music composition is no more complicated. Pick instruments, write a rhythm, a melody, a chord progression or a bass line for each of them, mix and match, and voilà! No seriously, it is hardly more complicated than that, it just takes some time, but for a starting point that is pretty much it.

So, ready to get started? Then see you on the first chapter!

PS: You can head over to hackmusictheory.com to find additional resources. They have a great free tutorial to get started with music theory.
