10 - Mastering: The Last Touch

01/13/2025 Categories: Mixing, Mastering

We are almost there. We wrote our song, we applied effects to our instruments, we balanced it all. Now is the time to add the last touch before pressing the export button!

Mastering the easy way: automated mastering

Some convenient tools exist if you do not want to bother with yet another layer of messing up with effects and settings. BandLab offers such an option (and that's the only option they offer anyway!). If you go to your library tab in BandLab, select your track, then click on the three dots next to the "Studio" button, you should see a "Apply Mastering" option in the contextual menu.

You should then be presented with a handful but limited list of presets (only three of them are free), as well as a range input that lets you choose the intensity of the mastering applied. If you don't know what to pick, you can keep the default settings. And that's it, your song is ready for download!

Some DAW plugins like Ozone also offer automated mastering, but they are usually not free (sometimes you get a license when buying some physical devices).

Mastering the hard way: effects on the master channel (OPTIONAL)

I won't go in details here because, to be honest, I suck at mastering. I can however give you some generic guidelines, although there again I suggest to dive into tutorials online.

The first thing you can do is apply a limiter on the master channel to prevent peaks from saturating the audio (which can sound absolutely awful on some devices, we are not talking about the effect here).

Before the limiter (which is more harsh), you can add a compressor to make a smoother first pass and "buff" the mix (which is recommended on EDM music).

You may also use an equalizer to adjust the overall frequencies, and give a particular feeling to the song.

Finally, I suggest looking for mastering for a specific genre, as sometimes it is quite codified or characteristic of how it sounds.

Well, there is one last important step to finish our song: give it a name! Something that defines what it is about, what it reminds you, where your inspiration comes from, whatever you want that represents your song. For me, that will be "walking under the rain".

Wrapping up

Ok, that was quite intensive! So far we discovered the "joy" of music theory, wrote a drum loop, a chord progression, a melody, and a bass line. From this loop, we made a full song, where each instrument gets its own effects and space in the room. And we just added the last touch. Crazy, right?

You were probably lost many times, because I went to the essentials, and because we moved fast. And at the same time, I wrote some lengthy posts, and I apologize again for that. But I hope that through this little journey, you have now a better picture at how we can make a song from scratch (even if you didn't follow along!). As I said, it is not complicated in itself, but like drawing, there is a lot to learn, and you won't make the next world masterpiece on day one. But everyone can master it if they pour enough time into it! It just seems inaccessible because the vocabulary and tools are unconventional. It is just a barrier to cross, and I hope I helped you to cross it (or at least have a look on the other side of the wall!).
